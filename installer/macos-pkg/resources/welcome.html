Welcome to Mining Guardian

Bitcoin mining-fleet observability and auto-remediation, running entirely on your hardware.

What this installer does

  • Lays down the Mining Guardian application at /usr/local/MiningGuardian.
  • Stands up a local Postgres 16 database in a Colima-managed container, bound to 127.0.0.1:5432 only.
  • Installs Ollama and pulls a local LLM model sized to your Mac's RAM (16 GB → llama3.2:3b, 24 GB+ → qwen2.5:14b-instruct-q4_K_M).
  • Registers four background services so Mining Guardian comes up automatically every time this Mac boots.
One network step at first install: the local LLM model is pulled from ollama.com the first time you run this installer. After install, Mining Guardian operates entirely offline — no cloud-only dependencies, no telemetry, nothing leaves your network.

What you'll need

  • An Apple Silicon Mac (M-series) running macOS 13 (Ventura) or later.
  • At least 16 GB of RAM and 20 GB of free disk on the boot volume.
  • Network access for the one-time Ollama model pull (~2 GB at 16 GB tier, ~9 GB at 24 GB+ tier).
  • Your Mac's administrator password — Installer.app will ask for it when you click Install.

What happens after Install

Once the installer finishes, the four Mining Guardian services start immediately and a baseline scan runs in the background. You can open the dashboard at http://127.0.0.1:8080/ within about 30 seconds.
