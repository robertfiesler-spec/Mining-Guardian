Mining Guardian · v1.0

Welcome

Bitcoin mining-fleet observability and auto-remediation, running entirely on your hardware. No cloud, no telemetry, no shared tenancy.

What this installer does

  • Lays down the Mining Guardian application at /Library/Application Support/MiningGuardian.
  • Stands up a local Postgres 16 database in a Colima-managed container, bound to 127.0.0.1:5432 only.
  • Installs Ollama and pulls a local LLM model sized to your Mac's RAM (16 GB › llama3.2:3b; 24 GB+ › qwen2.5:14b-instruct-q4_K_M).
  • Registers ten background services so Mining Guardian comes up automatically every time this Mac boots.
  • Registers eleven scheduled jobs (nightly briefings, weekly training, hourly benchmarks, log collection) using the macOS-native launchd scheduler — no Full Disk Access prompt, no extra setup.
One network step at first install. The local LLM model is pulled from ollama.com the first time you run this installer. After that, Mining Guardian operates entirely offline — no cloud-only dependencies, no telemetry, nothing leaves your network.

What you'll need

  • An Apple Silicon Mac (M-series) running macOS 13 (Ventura) or later.
  • At least 16 GB of RAM and 20 GB of free disk on the boot volume.
  • Network access for the one-time Ollama model pull (~2 GB at the 16 GB tier; ~9 GB at the 24 GB+ tier).
  • Your Mac's administrator password — Installer.app will ask for it when you click Install.
  • The pre-filled MiningGuardian.conf on this Mac's Desktop — the Mining Guardian operator will hand it to you. The installer reads it once at install time and writes its values into the local .env; nothing leaves the Mac.

What happens after install

Once the installer finishes, the ten Mining Guardian services start immediately and a baseline scan runs in the background. You can open the dashboard at http://127.0.0.1:8585/ and the operator console at http://127.0.0.1:8787/ within about 30 seconds.
