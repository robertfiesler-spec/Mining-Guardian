## Notes on Nomenclature

- **Avalon1 Batch 1/2/3** = the original 2013 open-source hardware shipped in three batches.
- From **Avalon 741** onwards Canaan used three-digit numbers for the mainstream (non-consumer) line.
- From **A1246** onwards the naming shifted to the "A1xxx" format.
- From **A15/A1566** the series letter prefix changed to A15, A16 etc.
- **Consumer line**: Nano 3, Nano 3S (desktop heaters), Mini 3 (panel heater), Avalon Q (home rack).
- Chip nomenclature: A3256 (110 nm) → A3255 (55 nm) → A3233/A3222 (40 nm) → A3218 (28 nm) → A3210/A3212 (16 nm) → A3205 (16 nm, 2nd gen) → A3202/A3204/A3206/A3207/A3210HP (7 nm) → A12 (7 nm, 2020–21) → A3246 (7 nm, A13/14 series) → A15 (4 nm, 2024–25) → A16 (process TBD, Samsung, 2025).
- **Efficiency** = Power ÷ Hashrate; values rounded to 1 decimal.
- "Hashboards" = number of hashboard PCBs inside the unit.
- Cooling types: **Air** (standard fans), **Imm** (immersion liquid), **Hydro** (water/liquid cold-plate, 2U rack format).

## Detailed Notes by Model

### Gen 1 — Avalon1 (2013)
- **Chip**: A3256, TSMC 110 nm process, 295 MH/s per chip
- **Configuration**: The original miner shipped in modules of 80 chips each; 2-module (160 chips, 60 GH/s, 595 W), 3-module (240 chips, 72 GH/s, 600 W), or 4-module (320 chips, 90 GH/s, 750 W). Bitcoin Wiki records "Batch 1/2/3" all as 66 GH/s @ 620 W (standard 2+2 module config at launch); later batches ran slightly higher.
- **Sources**: [Bitcoin Wiki Avalon1](https://en.bitcoin.it/wiki/Avalon1), [Bitcoin Wiki Mining Hardware Comparison](https://en.bitcoin.it/wiki/Mining_hardware_comparison)

### Gen 2 — Avalon2 (2013–14)
- **Chip**: A3255, 55 nm, 1.5 GH/s per chip
- **Hashrate**: 315 GH/s (normal), 210 GH/s (ECO mode)
- **Power**: 1,020 W (normal), 420 W (ECO)
- **Source**: [Bitcoin Wiki Avalon2](https://en.bitcoin.it/wiki/Avalon2)

### Gen 3 — Avalon3 (2014)
- **Chip**: A3233, 40 nm, 7.08 GH/s per chip, 600 MHz
- **Hashrate**: ~800 GH/s
- **Source**: [Bitcoin Wiki Avalon3](https://en.bitcoin.it/wiki/Avalon3)

### Gen 4 — Avalon4 / Avalon4.1 (2014)
- **Chip**: A3222, 40 nm (per [zorinaq.com electricity analysis](http://blog.zorinaq.com/bitcoin-electricity-consumption/))
- **Avalon 4.1** ships with 40 A3233 chips, 1.3 TH/s, ~0.7 W/GH — the A3233 is a later 40 nm variant
- **Source**: [CryptoCompare Avalon 4.1](https://www.cryptocompare.com/mining/avalon/avalon-41-btc-mining-equipment-13-ths/)

### Gen 6 — Avalon6 (2015)
- **Chip**: A3218, 28 nm (Canaan gave zorinaq.com explicit data that A3218 was in Avalon 6, achieving 0.29 J/GH; launched November 2015)
- **Specs**: 3.5 TH/s, ~1,000–1,080 W
- **Source**: [blog.zorinaq.com](http://blog.zorinaq.com/bitcoin-electricity-consumption/), [Coinfunda](https://coinfunda.com/best-bitcoin-mining-hardware/)

### Gen 7 — Avalon 721 / 741 / 761 (2016–17)
- **Chip**: A3212, 16 nm, per [CryptoCompare](https://www.cryptocompare.com/mining/canaan-creative/avalonminer-741-sha256-73ths/) and [Fruugo listing](https://www.fruugo.us/new-avalon-canaan-for-mining-auc3-converter/p-129902718)
- **721**: 6.0 TH/s, ~900 W — released Nov 2016
- **741**: 7.3 TH/s, 1,150 W, 88 × A3212 chips — released Apr 2017
- **761**: 8.8 TH/s, 1,320 W, 104 × A3212 chips — released 2017

### Gen 8 — Avalon 821 / 841 / 851 / 852 (2018–19)
- **821 chip**: A3210 (16 nm), 104 chips, 4 hashboards — per [BitcoinTalk review](https://bitcointalk.org/index.php?topic=2937129)
- **841 chip**: A3210HP (16 nm, improved A3210) — per [BitcoinTalk review](https://bitcointalk.org/index.php?topic=3191702.0)
- **851**: 14.5 TH/s, 1,450 W
- **852**: 15.0 TH/s, 1,500 W

### Gen 9 — Avalon 910 / 911 / 920 / 921 (2018–19)
- **921 chip**: A3207, 7 nm — per [Bitcoin Merch](https://bitcoinmerch.com/products/canaan-avalonminer-921-profitability-and-payback)
- Note: 910/920 are believed to use A3207 as well; some secondary sources place them in 2018–19 as the A9 series

### Gen 10 — Avalon 1026 / 1041 / 1047 / 1066 / 1066 Pro (2019–20)
- **Chip**: A3205, 16 nm — per [D-Central guide](https://d-central.tech/manuals/avalonminer-1066-maintenance-repair-guide/)
- **1041**: 240 × A3205 chips; 31 TH/s @ 1,736 W — per [BlokForge](https://blokforge.com/product/canaan-avalon-1041-31th-s-bitcoin-miner/)
- **1066**: 342 × A3205 chips (114 per board × 3 boards) — per D-Central
- **1066 Pro**: 342 × A3205 chips; 55 TH/s — per [2140miner.com](https://www.2140miner.com/avalon-1066-pro-55ths-product/)

### Gen 11 — Avalon 1126 Pro / 1146 / 1146 Pro / 1166 / 1166 Pro (2020–21)
- **Chip**: A3202 (also identified as K210 on control board listings — K210 is the AI control SoC, not the hash chip; hash chip is A3202), 7 nm
- **1126 Pro bins**: 60, 64, 68 TH/s @ 3,420 W — per [BT-Miners](https://bt-miners.com/products/canaan-avalonminer-1126-pro-68-th-s-bt-miners/)
- **1146 Pro**: 63 TH/s @ 3,276 W — per [Hashrate Index](https://hashrateindex.com/rigs/canaan-avalonminer-1146-pro-63th)
- **1166 Pro bins**: 72, 75, 78, 81 TH/s — multiple sources confirm different boards produce different bins
- Note: The 1166 Pro firmware file is labeled "A1166Pro-A3202" confirming the A3202 chip name

### Gen 12 — Avalon A1246 / A1266 (2020–22)
- **Chip**: "A12" (7 nm TSMC) — per [Newegg listing](https://www.newegg.com/canaan-avalonminer-1246/p/37E-028F-00005)
- **A1246 bins**: 83, 85, 90, 93, 96 TH/s — all at 3,420 W; the 96 TH bin shipped first (Dec 2020); lower bins shipped from Jan 2021
- **A1266**: 100 TH/s, 3,500 W — per [Hashrate Index](https://hashrateindex.com/rigs/canaan-avalonminer-1266)

### Gen 13 — Avalon A1326 / A1346 / A1366 / A1366I (2022–23)
- **Chip**: A3246, 7 nm
- **A1326**: 106–115 TH/s @ 3,350–3,500 W — per [Minetheasic](https://minetheasic.com/canaan-avalonminer-a1326)
- **A1366I**: immersion model; two variants (119 TH and 122 TH at 3,570 W; 165 TH at higher OC)

### Gen 14 — Avalon A1446 / A1466 / A1466I (2023)
- **Chip**: A3246 (improved bin)
- **A1466I**: immersion; 153 TH and 170 TH bins at 3,315–3,320 W — per [Canaan Official Shop](https://shop.canaan.io/products/avalon-miner-a1466i-170t-immersion-cooling-future)

### Gen 15 — Avalon A1566 / A1566I / A1566HA / A15 series (2024–25)
- **Chip**: A15, 4 nm — per [X-ON Mining](https://xonmining.com/product/canaan-avalon-a1566-bitcoin-miner/) and [Coin Mining Central](https://coinminingcentral.com/products/canaan-avalon-a15pro-221-bitcoin-miner-221-th-s)
- **A1566**: 185 TH/s, 3,420 W, air, 3 hashboards — per [Hashrate Index review](https://hashrateindex.com/blog/avalon-a1566-asic-miner-testing-and-review/)
- **A1566I**: immersion; 249 TH and 261 TH bins — per [Zeus Mining](https://www.zeusbtc.com/Asic-Miner/Asic-Miner-Details.asp?ID=3617) and [Coin Mining Central](https://coinminingcentral.com/products/canaan-avalon-a1566i-immersion-cooling-bitcoin-miner-240-270th-s)
- **A1566HA 2U**: hydro (water-cooled rack module), 480 TH/s, 8,064 W — per [Canaan Official Shop](https://shop.canaan.io/products/avalon-miner-a1566ha-480t)
- **A15 (194T)**: 194 TH/s, 3,647 W — per ASIC Miner Value
- **A15 Pro**: ships as 218 TH and 221 TH bins @ 3,662 W — per [Canaan Official](https://shop.canaan.io/products/avalon-miner-a15pro-221t)
- **A15XP**: 206 TH/s, 3,667 W — per [ASIC Marketplace](https://asicmarketplace.com/product/canaan-avalon-a15xp-bitcoin-miner/)

### Gen 16 — Avalon A16 / A16XP (2025–26)
- **Chip**: A16, manufactured at Samsung (process node not yet publicly disclosed) — per [Hashrate Index A16 review](https://hashrateindex.com/blog/hardware-review-canaans-a16-series/)
- **A16**: 282 TH/s, 3,900 W, 13.8 J/TH — announced Oct 2025
- **A16XP**: 300 TH/s, 3,850 W, 12.8 J/TH — announced Oct 2025

### Consumer Line
- **Avalon Nano 3** (2024): 4 TH/s, 140 W — powered by 10 integrated chips; USB-C, Wi-Fi — per [Hashrate Index Nano 3 review](https://hashrateindex.com/blog/canaan-avalon-nano-3-your-humble-heater-for-home-mining/)
- **Avalon Nano 3S** (2025): 6 TH/s, 140 W, 23.3 J/TH — 50% more hashrate than Nano 3 via next-gen chip
- **Avalon Mini 3** (2025): 37.5 TH/s, 800 W, 21.3 J/TH — 66 chips, 4 nm process, dual-use space heater form factor
- **Avalon Q** (2025): 90 TH/s, 1,674 W, 18.6 J/TH, 3 hashboards — rack-style home miner with LCD and app control
